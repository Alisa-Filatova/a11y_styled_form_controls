Switch Toggle Component: Checkbox

Pattern to restyle a checkbox into a switch component.

The switch components are coded with the following markup patterns: 

<!-- Version: [type="checkbox"] styled to look like a switch -->
<label class="check-switch" for="switch_1">
  <input type="checkbox" id="switch_1" role="switch">
  <span tabindex="-1">Example Text:</span>
  <!--
    the tabindex="-1" is here to negate VO
    from announcing the "example" label twice
  -->
</label>

<!-- Version: [type="checkbox"][role="switch"] -->
<label class="check-switch" for="switch_2">
  <input type="checkbox" id="switch_2" role="switch">
  <span tabindex="-1">Example Text:</span>
  <!--
    the tabindex="-1" is here to negate VO
    from announcing the "example" label twice
  -->
</label>

<!-- Alternate markup version -->
<div class="switchbox">
  <label for="switchbox">
    Label Text:
  </label>
  <input type="checkbox" id="switchbox" role="switch">
</div>

The typical user expectation for a checkbox is for it to be engaged with, and then for an additional action, such as submitting a form, to be necessary for the updated state of the checkbox to be recognized by the website/web app.

e.g. a user checking or unchecking an "add me to your mailing list" field. The action of adding or being removed does not transpire until the form is submitted.

As this version of a switch toggle will be announced by assistive technologies as a "checkbox", the best place to use this pattern would be in the context of a series of form fields that were to be submitted all at once. Or as a switch toggle that was coupled with a "Save" or "Submit" button.
